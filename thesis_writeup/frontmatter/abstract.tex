Classical evolutionary optimisation of steel trusses is well understood
but computationally expensive: each candidate design requires a full
finite-element (FEM) solve, and thousands of candidates are evaluated
per run. This thesis develops a unified computational framework that
accelerates and augments classical truss optimisation using modern
artificial-intelligence techniques, and validates the framework on four
canonical benchmark trusses (10-bar, 25-bar, 72-bar, and 200-bar) under
the Indian Standard IS~800:2007 design code.

The framework stacks eight layers on top of a custom FEM engine
validated to \num{e-21} precision. Classical genetic algorithm (GA),
particle swarm optimisation (PSO), and non-dominated sorting genetic
algorithm~II (NSGA-II) wrappers provide reference baselines. A neural
multi-layer-perceptron surrogate is trained to predict weight,
displacement, and stress response, reducing the inner-loop evaluation
cost by two orders of magnitude. A proximal-policy-optimisation (PPO)
reinforcement-learning agent is trained on the surrogate environment
and demonstrates a direct policy-based alternative to evolutionary
search. A large-language-model (LLM) warm-start interface translates
natural-language design briefs into initial area vectors, yielding a
statistically significant reduction in generations-to-convergence on
the 10-bar benchmark.
